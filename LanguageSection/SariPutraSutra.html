Sariputra Sutra

CKM 367

[Na me diṭha ido puve,
Na suto uda kaspacid;
Evo vaguvada saṭ́ha,
Tusitā ganigata.]
Not seen before by me,
nor heard by anyone:
such sweetly-spoken Teacher
from Tusita came to lead a group.
[Sadevakasa lokasa,
yasa diśati cakṣumaṃta;
Savo tamo vinudatva,
ekova radim asiga.]
One by himself attained to bliss,
all darkness he dispelled,
so that the One-With-Eyes be seen
by world together with the gods.
[to buddho asita tadi,
akuho ganima agada;
Bahunamidha badhana,
athi praśeña agama.]
One’s who’s “Thus”, the unattached,
that Buddha undeceptive,
with many disciples, devotees,
for them I ask a question.
[bhikhuno vijiguchata,
bhajata ritamasana;
rukṣamula spaśano va,
pavadana guhasu va.]
For a monk avoiding society,
seeking out a lonely place—
bone yards, at the base of trees,
or caves within the mountain wastes—
[ucavayeṣu śayaneṣu,
keti tatra bherava;
yehi bhikhu na cala'e,
tuṣibhava śenasane.]
Living-places high or low,
How many are the terrors there,
that a monk in his silent place
trembles not at all?
[keti parisrava loke,
gachaṃta agada disa;
yo ya bhikhu avhisaṃbhave,
praṃta hi śenasane.]
How many are the troubles here
for a monk to overcome,
while living in a place remote,
or going to the Ungone-Point.
[kasa veṃjana siya,
kasa siya itha gocara;
kani śilavada siya,
prahidatva bhikhuna.]
What ways of speaking would be his?
What place should he frequent?
What sorts of rules, kinds of vows,
For the monk with mind intent?
[ko so śikṣa samadaya,
ekodi nipaga spada;]
kamara rayidaṃseva,
nidhame malaṃ [atvaṇa].
(Same wording in Split Collection 3)
What is the training he adopts,
one-pointed, mindful, wise;
to blow away all blemishes,
as does a smith with silver?
[vijigucchamānasa yadidaṁ phaṣa,
ritasana sayana sevata ce;
saṃbosa kamasa yasa anudharma,
to te pavakṣati yasa prajana.]
As One who Knows I’ll explain to you,
what’s pleasant for you practicing avoidance,
who live and who rest in a lonely abode,
wishing Awakening in keeping with Dharma.
[pacana dhira bhayano na bhae,
Bhikhu spada sapayaṃtacari;
ḍaṁsadhipadana sarisapana,
manuśaphaṣana catupadana.]
Within limits the mindful monk practices,
then of five fears is this wise one not afraid:
March-flies and mosquitoes, of slithering snakes,
of men’s assaults, and fierce four-footed beasts.
[paradharmikanapi na achaṃbi,
diṭha api teso bahubheravani;
asavara avhisambhave'a,
pariṣayani kuśala anueṣi.]
Nor be disturbed by those with differing Dharma,
even having seen their many perils,
further then, this seeker of the good
will overcome all fearfulness too.
aṯagaphaṣeṣo kṣus̱ae phuṭho,
śiṯo vas̱ana pi as̱ivas̱aeha.
ede[ṣ]o phuṭho bahus̱o anvayo,
virya parakrama diḍha karea.
Afflicted by illness and hunger,
they should endure cold and excessive heat.
Though afflicted by many such things, the homeless one
should exert energy, firmly striving.
[coridaga na kare'a na muṣa bhaṇe'a,
metra'a phaṣe trasaṭ́havaraṇi;
yada avilatra manaso vijaṇe'a,
kiṣa pakṣa ti vinudaye'a.]
Neither should he steal, nor should he tell lies,
but let love suffuse the fearful and the unafraid,
and when his mind is agitated let him know
they drive out what is dark.
kros̱as̱a manas̱a vaśo ma gache,
[mulovi teso palikhaṃña tiṭhadi.
asa priya va vana apiyo va,
adha bhonto avhisambhave'a.]
They must not fall under the sway of anger or arrogance;
having dug them out by the root, they would stand firm.
Then, withstanding likes and dislikes,
that they would overcome.
[paṃña puraṭ́hitva kalanapridi,
vikhaṃbha'e tani pariṣayani; aradi sahetha sayaṃpi paṃde,
caduro sahetha parideva dharme.]
Putting wisdom in the foremost place, rejoicing in goodness,
they would put an end to those adversities.
They’d vanquish discontent in their remote lodging.
And they’d vanquish the four lamentations:
[kiṃsu aśaśami kuva va aśaśami,
Dukha vada śeda kvaja śedi;
Ete vitake paridevane'e,
niharadi śikṣa anagariyacara.]
Alas, what shall I eat and where indeed eat it,
last night l slept badly and where sleep today—
one-in-training, a wanderer, of no flag the follower
should such thoughts let go, leading to lamentation.
[aṃna ya ladha vasana ya kale,
matra so ñanā ido toṣa na aṭ́ha;
So teṣu gupto yatacara grame,
rusito api vaya pharuṣa na vaja.]
Satisfied, receiving timely food and clothes,
knowing moderation in them, and
protected by them, in a village he’s restrained
though roughly he’s addressed, speaks no harsh word.
[okṣitacakṣu na ca padalola,
jana anuyuta bahu jagaraṃta;
uvekṣamaravha samahidatva,
takaśaya kukurvi'a upachiṃdida.]
With eyes cast down, feet not longing-guided,
to jhāna devoted, very watchful he should be,
let him grow in equanimity with mind composed,
check his scruples, how he inclines to doubt.
[codida vayehi spadi avhinaṃde,
saha braṃmacarena akhila pabhiṃde;
vaya pramuṃca kuśala nadivela,
janavadadhasmaya na cetaye'a.]
With words of reproof let the mindful one rejoice,
and shatter his scorn for his fellow-celibates;
and utter skilful words at the proper time,
and think not upon views and beliefs of common folk.
[asavara paca rajani loke,
yesa spadima venae'a śikṣe;
ruveṣu chadeṣu asa raseṣu,
gaṃdheṣu phaṣeṣu sahetha raga.]
And then in the world, there are the dusty five
in which the mindful one guided, trains himself well,
lust overcoming to bodies and to sounds,
to tastes, to perfumes and touches too.
[edeṣu dharmeṣu vine'a chaṃda,
bhikhu satima suvimutacito;
kalena so saṃma dharma parivimaṃsamano,
Ekodibhuda vihane tama so di.]
And when in these things he has guided desire,
mindful, that bhikkhu of a well-liberated mind,
then he in due time thoroughly examining Dharma,
with mind become one he shall the darkness rend.
